ARCF - Augmented Reality Based Carbon Footprint Labelling

Author: Denver Saldanha

Date: 31-08-2022

Abstract: This project focuses on designing a Carbon Footprint Label (a.k.a. Eco-Label) for sustainable products and creating awareness for the use of such products. This project Initially deals with understanding the history of HCI for sustainability and then comes to studying the multiple past attempts of developing a CF Label by several organizations and governments and why some passed and why some failed. The second phase involves defining Carbon Footprint Labels in order to come up with a better and more innovative approach to Carbon Footprint Labels while keeping the previous research in the foreground. The medium of interaction used in this project is AR (Augmented Reality) which aims to encompass the physical world with your mobile phone screen and camera to work together to make the customer aware of the environmental benefits and drawbacks of the products that they buy during a visit to the supermarket. In turn, allowing them to make more informed choices. Therefore, the research question is, “How can Carbon Footprint Labelling help customers in making more environmentally friendly purchases for FMCG products through Augmented Reality (AR)?”

Softwares Used: Unity 2021, Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Vuforia

The Goal: The goal of this project was to create an android app that would help customers choose and compare products based on thier environmental friendliness on a vist to the supermarket.

Conclusion: When it comes to deciding the outcome of this project, it could be determined by analysing the initial research question asked. The main goal of this project was to influence customers to purchase more environmentally friendly products by using Augmented Reality (AR) as the method of interaction. The evaluation of the experimental approach suggests that there is a substantial amount of people who would choose a product with lesser Carbon emissions. However, the price remains a big differentiating factor that could tend to sway the purchasing decision in either possible direction. Other factors such as the quality of the product and awareness raised about the label could also potentially cause negative repercussions on the success of the label. The augmentation of data and its visualization implemented in the Carbon Footprint label draws valuable interest in persuading customers to check out the Carbon Footprint of a product in a supermarket-like setting while the animation used as a part of the augmentation helps the customers understand the label more advantageously. The easy-to-use interface on the app makes it easy for customers to scan products while they are shopping in a supermarket. While it is known that the willingness to pay is drastically reduced in a few groups of people, this project would help people who place environmental issues over financial frugality to make more eco-friendly decisions.

Submitted: For the degree of Master of Science in Interaction & Experience Design at the University of Limerick 2021-2022
