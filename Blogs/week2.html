Week 2 - Cultural Heritage

Author: Denver Saldanha

Date: 11-02-2022

Introduction: Welcome to my Blog, this is one of the first Blogs of a series in which I go through all my coursework and experiences learning about various technologies and real-life applications of it used in our surroundings. I will try to list down key points of my interest and some learnings that helped me enlighten myself during the 13 weeks of this module. I wouldn't call myself the best of writers since most of my works are best portrayed through digital art. However, Listing down my learnings would help me reflect back on this module and my understanding of topics that now seem to run in the background sometimes. Our course leader Gabriella Avram guided us through all these sessions and helped us better understand the sound and knowledgeable use of technology and in some cases technologies that I didn't even know existed. So let's grab a cup of coffee ☕ and get started.

This week's session consisted of the Introduction to Cultural Probes which are used across various sectors to understand people and their use of technology with a constantly changing variable which would be "culture" itself. Culture is ever-evolving and has a lot of interesting facts and knowledge that would be like a cherry on the top for a History nerd like me.

Cultural Probes

Cultural heritage could be defined as the sum of all Tangible and Intangible elements that could be credited to a section of the society. Cultural heritage includes tangible culture (such as buildings, monuments, landscapes, books, works of art, and artifacts), intangible culture (such as folklore, traditions, language, and knowledge), and natural heritage (including culturally significant landscapes, and biodiversity).

Technology as an enabler of Culture

Technology is very diverse and has many forms which is very similar to Culture in a way. Technology could be used as a means to transform society and bring about massive changes and explore various ideas that the human brain can't even comprehend. This module is all about linking technology to things happening around us and to become more aware of the use of technology in our surroundings. In this week's coursework we explored various cultures and tried to break down the technology used behind it. The potential of using technology to bridge gaps such as language, race, religion and other aspects of culture are immense. In some cases today's technology could become tomorrow's cultural heritage. Hence being a visionary becomes very important, so that we can foresee the future and build our technology likewise using it for good and purposeful work. Technology gives us a vision and a dream to make things more seamless and enjoyable for the future generations.

This week's Exercise

Our IXUX Class is very diverse and has students from a variety of cultures and backgrounds. This gave us an opportunity to share our experiences with each other and getting to know more about various cultures always widens your perspective about life. I was personally impressed by the various places shared by my classmates. We had many ecological heritage sites as well as historical structures that were breathtaking, shared with us. however two Nigerian students speaking about Slave houses stuck out to me the most. It just made me feel absolutely privileged to be born in a country like India where we as a country, never had to face the dark ages like other African Countries. It gave me a sense of how almost every country has had its dark periods and it was the perseverance shown by it's people that got them out of it. We also learnt from these examples how technology was used to enhance the experience of visitors. Sometimes where words fall short mediums like audio/visuals help in portraying the sentiments attached to the heritage site.

Screenshot of Class Mural showing various cultural Heritage places

My Example

In my exapmle, I spoke about the Global Vipasana Pagoda, because it was one such instance in which I felt the most at peace while visiting a site of Cultural Heritage. The Global Vipasana Pagoda is a large Dome structure that is used as a meditation hall for Buddhist Monks and people who wish to try out various meditation techniques passed down by Gautama Buddha himself. The first and largest dome was completed when bone relics of Gautama Buddha were enshrined in the central locking stone of the dome on October 29, 2006, making it the world's largest hollow stone masonry structure containing relics of the Buddha.
