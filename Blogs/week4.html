Week 4 - Wearable Tech

Author: Denver Saldanha

Date: 25-02-2022

Introduction: As weeks pass by, the course keeps getting more demanding and there are even more things piling up in front of me. This week was another such example of it. But amid all the assignments, the design workshop acts like a way to learn something out of the box that is also really creative most of the time. Having a design workshop once in a week feels like a refereshing experienece and also acts like a bonding session for all our classmates. This week brought forth a lot of challenegs but at the same time even more creativity from all of us. While I squeeze the last drop of creativity from myself, things just keep getting more challenging. In this blog I go though the design activity for this week that was quite innovative and new to me. This week was all about wearable technology. It is quite fascinating to me that how technology has come to exist even in fabrics that could be worn on your body. Wearable tech is not only smart watches and health devices that can attach to your body, but it could also be other technologies, for example, textiles that you can actually wear instead of having to strap onto your body.

Wearable Technology

What is Wearable technology?

Wearable technologies, known mostly just as “wearables,” are electronic devices that are physically worn by individuals in order to track, analyze and transmit personal data. These “smart” IoT devices can track biometric data from heart rate to sleep patterns, and are also becoming popular consumer technologies in the gaming and fashion industries.

This week's Activity

For this week's activity we used conductive thread and an LED to stitch together an LED circuit that lights up when the battery is inserted. This week's exercise was pretty fun and it was quite challenging as well.This is because I have always done circuits using traditional wire and never done it using conductive wire. Most of us coming into this course never imagined that we would be handed a thread and needle to stretch something. But I guess if it's technology at the end then everyone's a winner. Here are a few images below illustrating the process.

We all found this activity really fun and used it as a way to express our creativity. Even though we were just stictching a thread based circuit, most people were really pumped since it was the first time people were putting together a circuit or using a thread and needle to stitch. When you do an activity like this, it just uplifts your mood when you see the light glow up after putting the last stitch on the cloth. Until the last stich is put onto the cloth, you never know if you have done your circuit right or not and I guess that encompasses this activity.
