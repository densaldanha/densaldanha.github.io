Week 5 - Designing for the Planet

Author: Denver Saldanha

Date: 04-03-2022

Introduction: The major theme in developing for the future is linked with sustainable ways to develop products that already exist in a more sustainable way. The earth has become a box full of the unused tools that we have kept in a corner of our house, it's gathering dust, but we hope to use them someday, but that day never really comes. Our earth has been left to the mercy of governments and big tech giants who don't seem to move an eye toward this issue. All these are linked together and one downfall or wrong decision by a government can have a domino effect on all these other blocks of society. We think that we have time and this thought has most people grounded towards not making any changes to the way they live.

After the lecture concluded we were left thinking about the disastrous things in the environments that surround us and how we need to act in time to save our planet. This brought us to a very fun and competitive game called the "Perspectivity Game". This game revolves around the basic concepts of sustainability and its effects on capitalism.

The Perspectivity Game

Every team needed to make a conscious choice of developing traditional factories or sustainable ones. We did not know the implications at the start of the game, they were only revealed at the end when we had our scores on display. The game is all about having the mindset of making more money but at the same time questioning the ethical implications of our decisions. There were multiple decisions to be made and strategies that we could choose to follow. Most of the groups just went on the route to earn more money and gain more command over the board, while some took the conservative approach not knowing what the implications would be. Every few minutes there was a disaster that came upon the earth that would wipe off a few factories or cause other damage, that would force us to choose another route to achieve our goals.

At the end of the game, we realised how each team was directly or indirectly linked to each other in affecting the ecosystem. This made us realise how capitalism is such an inseparable part of business and everyone wants to earn money at the end of the day. Striking a balance can be hard, but that is what we need at this very crucial time while saving our planet.

Overall this was a very fun game to play because it was really competitive and tactical. And at the same time, this game has a lot to teach us in the process of us playing it. This was definitely one of the most fun sessions that we have had in the design workshop sessions.

Keywords: Perspectivity game, game,activity,design,sustainability,tech,ixux design,business,capitalism.
