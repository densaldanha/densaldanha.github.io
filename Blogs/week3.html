Week 3 - Circular Design

Author: Denver Saldanha

Date: 18-02-2022

Introduction: Welcome to my blog yet again, this week, we had a chance to go through Circular Design and its implications in building better for a Circular economy. Amongst all the chaos with completing assignments to pressing the send button on my mail at the very last minute, I find myself trying to understand concepts and improve my knowledge of design and keep my self-esteem boosted throughout the week. I guess while walking on a thin rope the best you could do is keep your head straight focusing on your goal and not look down. Some concepts are more relatable than others and with us, all facing effects of Global Warming, concepts such as Circular Design become more crucial. Circular Design goes along with designing for the environment. "Saving the Environment" may sometimes seem like a very superficial term that is almost overused and over-glorified. Feels like it comes straight out of a Marvel movie that goes with a slogan of "Save the World!". Saying it might be easy but doing it practically might feel like an entire other Universe apart. The main goal for having a circular design focuses on reusing items and casting aside the utilizing and discarding culture. To be honest, this type of culture is very hard to discard since it goes hand in hand with other factors such as price and quality.

TReaching a stage where everyone uses renewable forms of energy or recycles and reuses everything they ever purchased may sound like a complete utopian scenario. Not everyone could afford what is packaged with a "sustainable future" since most of the time it is a hard to swallow pill for the working and middle-class society. That being said, it is argued that an ideal situation would involve designers embracing sustainability and having regard for the climate as a beginning stage.

Urban Mobility - Activity

Designing a product for Urban Mobility while keeping in mind the concept of circular design was quite challenging. Knowing the various factors involved while designing a product that keeps up with good sustainability standards, to start with, we came up with the key problems in Ireland in regards to Mobility.

  1. Limited Modes of Transportation
  2. Time Constraints.
  3. Same fare. (Affordable)
  4. Good After Sales Service.
  5. Traffic movement
  6. Limited parking space
  7. CO2 emissions

The most used or reliable means of transport in Ireland are only trains and buses. These could be quite limiting as well. With this in mind we came up with two possible solutions that would suit the Irish Market and at the same time foster a Circular Design they are:

  1. E-bike Renting Service

  2. Car Pooling Service

    3. Carpooling is a service in which a car owner can volunteer to drive people to their desired location if they both have the same destination. Carpooling aims to reduce CO2 levels by dropping the number of cars on the road. If carpooling is correctly implemented, it has the capacity to stop a lot of cars from hitting the road. Most people going to work just use their cars as it is one of the easiest means of transport. It gives people the flexibility to remain self-reliant. But all of this does come at a cost. A cost not paid off immediately by them, but at the environment at large. Every car that goes on the road contributes to Global carbon emissions.
